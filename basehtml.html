OUR MISSION:

Provide a platform of communication for the public majority who often feel uninvolved, and unheard in the political process.

"Words mean more than what is set down on paper. It takes the human voice to infuse them with deeper meaning."

We believe ideas and opinions matter from everyone, everywhere. In fact, at Politiview, we believe everyone should have a platform of communication to speak their mind.


PLATFORM:

Our website provides accountability and transparency for politicians and publicly elected officials. Users can review officials from the local to federal level, based on a variety of objective factors such as truthfulness, voting record, corruption, interaction with voters andspecial interest groups, policy choices, efficiency with time in term, etc.

MOST REVIEWED

Michelle Bachmann
Member of Congress
Read Bio

A Representative from Minnesota. She was a candidate for the Republican nomination in the 2012 U.S. presidential election, winning the Ames Straw Poll in August 2011 but dropping out in January 2012 after finishing in sixth place in the Iowa caucuses. Bachmann previously served in the Minnesota State Senate and is the first Republican woman to represent the state in Congress. She is a supporter of the Tea Party movement and a founder of the House Tea Party Caucus. Bachmann has been dubbed as the "Queen of the Tea Party".

Rand Paul
U.S. Senator
Read Bio

The junior United States Senator for Kentucky. He is a member of the Republican Party and the son of former U.S. Representative and presidential candidate Ron Paul of Texas. He first received national attention in 2008 when making political speeches on behalf of his father, who was campaigning for the Republican Party's nomination for president. During his father's final term in the house, he was the first United States senator to have served simultaneously with a parent in the United States House of Representatives.

Joe Biden
Vice President
Read Bio

As a Senator from Delaware for 36 years, Senator Biden established himself as a leader on some of our nation's most important domestic and international challenges. As Chairman or Ranking Member of the Senate Judiciary Committee for 17 years, then-Senator Biden was widely recognized for his work on criminal justice issues including the landmark 1994 Crime Bill and the Violence Against Women Act. As Chairman or Ranking Member of the Senate Foreign Relations Committee since 1997, then-Senator Biden played a pivotal role in shaping U.S. foreign policy.Now, as the 47th Vice President of the United States, Joe Biden has continued his leadership on important issues facing the nation.
